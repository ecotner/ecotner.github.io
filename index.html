  Eric Cotner, theoretical physicist and recent graduate of UCLA's PhD program. My work during this time was primarily related to theories of dark matter, cosmology, and exotic phenomena in the early Universe.
  Particle physics and ideas from quantum field theory are the primary tool and driving force behind discoveries in this field right now, and with the discovery of dark matter (unpredicted matter of an unknown identity), we may be on the cusp of a revolution in physics.
  Some of the things that are occupying my attention right now are the possible formation of lumps of dark matter from Bose-Einstein condensates known as Q-balls or oscillons, formation of primordial black holes from clustering of these objects, and (extra)galactic magnetogenesis from magnetized black hole accretion discs. In addition to more traditional pen-and-paper methods, I try to utilize the wonderful computing power at our disposal through numerical simulations whenever possible. This makes for some nice images and animations you can watch on my research page.
  I am also interested in machine learning, specifically neural networks and deep learning, and am trying to catch up with the current state-of-the-art in several sub-fields, such as computer vision and reinforcement learning. These are exciting times to be working in this area, as it seems success with applying deep learning to complicated problems is progressing at an exponential rate! Some things I'm currently working on involve reproducing several well-known algorithms, such as deep Q-learning to teach an agent to play Pong, object detection and image segmentation through convolutional neural networks, and some fanciful research on experimental optimization techniques.
