General Atomics Research

 General Atomics is a defense contractor and nuclear energy research corporation. Their nuclear fusion program aids in the development of everything from the ITER and D-III tokamaks to the National Ignition Facility's inertial fusion project. My research during my time at GA specifically was focused on the design and fabrication of prototype fuel injection and target tracking systems for use in the NIF's inertial fusion experiments. You can see more of what I did here.
 I have worked on many projects during my time at GA, all of which have been exciting research opportunities which I have benefited greatly from:

  • LIFE induction accelerator
    • Built an electromagnetic induction accelerator for launching hohlraums into ignition chamber with high speed/repeatability. (See our paper published by the American Nuclear Society)
  • LIFE target tracking system
    • Built a tracking system for predicting the flight path of a hohlraum to steer ignition lasers to hit target on the fly.
  • TNSA proton beam laser targets
    • Fabricated specific targets for converting laser energy into a collimated beam of protons. Potential use as an ignition source as an alternative to lasers. Overview of the project is here
  • Nickel microwave absorption compound
    • Created an organic solution with high microwave absorption to use maser-driven explosive thermal expansion as a means for delivering fuel pellets to the core plasma at D-III or ITER.
  • Polypropylene droplet generation
    • Experimented with mass production of small polypropylene spheres for use in shell fabrication.
  • Pb hohlraum deflashing
    • Experimented with a deflashing procedure for hohlraums using ultrasonic cavitation and vibratory media.
  • N2 dry box
    • Created an automated desiccator to keep shell samples under low humidity.
  • Vacuum gripper
    • Created robot tooling for handling hohlraums.
  • UV glue surface tension
    • Measured UV glue surface tension and contact angle on glass and diamond substrates to test feasibility of filling a shell layer with the glue and curing.
