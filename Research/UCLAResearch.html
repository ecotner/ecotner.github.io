UCLA Research

  I am currently working with Alexander Kusenko on questions of cosmological interest. Our primary subject of study is dark matter, an elusive form of matter that is transparent to electromagnetic interactions. There are numerous different candidates for dark matter, ranging from WIMPs to axions, sterile neutrinos, Q-balls, and primordial black holes. (Check out this news article about my research!)) We are also interested in other astrophysical phenomena such as magnetogenesis and baryogenesis. You can see some of my work in these areas below.

  • Solitons
    • Boson stars
    • Q-balls
  • Primordial Black Holes (PBH)
    • PBH from nontopological solitons in the early universe
