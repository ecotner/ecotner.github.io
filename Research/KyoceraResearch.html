Kyocera America Research

 Kyocera America is an electronics and semiconductor manufacturer which specializes in ceramic substrates for high-power RF applications where thermal dissapation is extremely important. They provide a variety of solutions to a wide base of customers (military, aerospace, consumer, etc.) and can manufacture a custom electronics package from start to finish. My research was mainly in failure analysis of substrates in their research and development lab, where I became well-versed in scanning electron microscopy, EDX spectroscopy, and x-ray element mapping. Some of the projects I have worked on include:

  • Flip chip solder analysis
    • Developed etching procedure to enhance grain boundaries and analyze intermetallic compounds in SnPb solder.
  • Nickel diffusion
    • Determined extent of nickel diffusion through gold plating on multiple shipments of packages using EDX spectroscopy.
  • Whisker formation
    • Determined the source of whiskers/nodules forming on the die attach area of a package. Discovered through lack of cobalt at the interface that pure nickel particles were nucleating in the electroplating solution and depositing themselves on the surface.
  • Pb-free solder feasibility
    • Determined ideal solder reflow profile for various Pb-free solders and characterized the intermetallic compounds and grain structure for different reflow profiles.
  • Substrate chipping defects
    • Determined the source of substrate defects on the assembly line by tracking several groups throughout the process and implementing inspections after every step. Determined the source of the damage and implemented a new handling procedure to reduce number of defective parts.
  • Ink adhesion study
    • Tested the adhesion strength of metallic inks for marking substrates amd developed a method to determine whether a contaminant had been introduced to the ink by measuring viscosity.
  • Substrate failure analysis
    • Prepared samples for both optical and scanning electron microscopy and analyzed them for layer delamination, via displacement, ruptured BGA balls, missing components, etc.
  • X-ray analysis of solder bridging
    • Determined the extent of solder bridging between solder pads under a hermetic seal using x-ray photography to take picture, then image analysis software to determine the extent of the bridging.
  • BGA moisture failure
    • Determined the effect of moisture exposure on ball grid array solder stability by exposing packages to varying levels of moisture, then subjecting them to different reflow profiles and analyzing the extent of the damage.
